Yves-Du-Marnoir Stadium

Yves-du-Manoir Stadium hosted rugby sevens, track and field and other sports during the 2024 Paris Olympics. Its versatility and historical significance made it a key venue for the Games.

Paris la Défense Arena - Nanterre

Paris La Défense Arena is a modern arena in Nanterre, France. It hosted basketball, handball, and gymnastics during the 2024 Summer Olympics.

Roland Garros Stadium

Roland Garros Stadium hosted the tennis events. Its clay courts, which are unique to the French Open, provided a challenging and exciting playing surface for the world's best tennis players.

Porte de Versailles

Porte de Versailles is a large exhibition center in Paris. It hosted table tennis, weightlifting, and taekwondo during the 2024 Summer Olympics.

Accor Arena

Accor Arena hosted the boxing events. Its spacious interior and state-of-the-art facilities provided a suitable environment for the intense competition.

Chateau de Versailles

Chateau de Versailles hosted the equestrian events. The palace's grand grounds and historical significance provided a unique and memorable setting for these prestigious competitions.

La Concorde

La Concorde is used to play Basketball, Skateboarding and BMX freestyle in this olympics.

Champs De Mars

Champs de Mars Arena (also known as Grand Palais Éphémère) is a temporary exhibition hall in the Champ de Mars, a large public greenspace in Paris. This stadium was also used to hold 9000 people.

Marseille

Marseille hosted the sailing events for the Olympics. The city's coastal location and favorable wind conditions made it an ideal venue for this sport. The sailing competitions took place in the Mediterranean Sea off the coast of Marseille.

Arc de Triomphe

Arc de Triomphe was used as a closing cerimony and gave respect to all the fallen french soldior and congradulated the atheletes that participated and won.

Aquatic Centre

The Aquatic Centre in Paris was specifically built to host swimming and diving events during the 2024 Summer Olympics. It was a temporary structure that was dismantled after the Games concluded.

Chapelle Arena

Porte de la Chapelle Arena is a multi-purpose indoor arena located in Paris, France. In the olympics basketball and handball matches were hosted in the stadium.

Le Bourget

Le Bourget is a suburb of Paris with a major airport. It likely played a supporting role in the 2024 Summer Olympics, such as transporting athletes or officials.

Arena Paris Nord

Arena Paris Nord is a multi-purpose indoor arena located in Villepinte, France. It's one of the largest arenas in Europe, with a capacity of around 17,000 spectators. In the olympics this was used as an area to train atheletes for the olympics.

About the

Olympics

The Olympics are a big sports competition that happens every four years.
Athletes from all over the world come together to compete in different sports like swimming, running, gymnastics, and more.
It's like a big sports festival where everyone cheers for their favorite athletes and countries. There are two kinds of Olympics: Summer Olympics and Winter Olympics.

The Summer Olympics have sports like swimming, running, and basketball, while the Winter Olympics have sports like skiing, ice skating, and snowboarding.
That means the biggest sports competition in the world will be happening in Paris, France.
People from all over the world will come to watch athletes compete in different sports.


The Destination

Paris

Paris is a beautiful city in France, known for its iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre Museum.
It's a popular tourist destination with its charming streets, historic architecture, and vibrant culture.
Paris is also a major center for fashion, art, and cuisine.
